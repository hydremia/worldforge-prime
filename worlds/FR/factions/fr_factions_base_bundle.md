## 1. Overview

**Full Name:** The Harpers  
**Common Names/Aliases:** Harpers, The Harpers of Twilight

**One-Sentence Summary:**
A loose, semi-secret network of idealistic agents working to preserve balance, oppose tyranny, and keep dangerous power from the reckless.

**Longer Summary:**
The Harpers are an old, semi-mythic organization of spies, bards, rangers, and mages who value freedom, knowledge, and subtle intervention over overt rule. Operating through small, independent cells, they quietly counter tyrants, cults, and apocalyptic threats by empowering local heroes and exposing corruption rather than seizing power themselves.

---
## 2. Origins & History (High-Level)

- Formed centuries ago in response to magical and political abuses across the North and Dalelands.
- Rose to prominence opposing tyrannical kingdoms and destructive magical cabals.
- Suffered schisms and purges but repeatedly re-formed, proving hard to truly destroy.
- In the 1490s DR, the Harpers are rebuilding, re-networking, and leveraging a new generation of agents.

---
## 3. Ideology, Goals & Methods

### 3.1 Ideology
- Power must be checked and balanced, especially arcane and political power.
- Knowledge should be preserved and used responsibly, not hoarded or suppressed.
- Ordinary people deserve freedom from tyrants, fiends, and dragons.

### 3.2 Goals
- Monitor and subtly disrupt tyrannical regimes and villainous factions.
- Safeguard powerful artifacts and lore from reckless use.
- Support local heroes and resistance movements rather than ruling directly.

### 3.3 Methods
- Covert intelligence gathering and information brokerage.
- Sabotage, exposure of scandals, and targeted strikes when necessary.
- Discreet patronage of adventuring parties who align with Harper values.

---
## 4. Structure & Organization

### 4.1 Leadership
- Guided by secret councils of senior Harpers and trusted high mages and bards.
- Leadership is decentralized; power is distributed to prevent co-option.

### 4.2 Internal Structure
- Organized into small, semi-autonomous cells.
- Use safe houses, inns, and shrines as informal meeting points.

### 4.3 Membership
- Bards, rangers, rogues, and mages are common.
- Members are vetted for idealism and discretion more than raw power.

---
## 5. Presence & Influence

### 5.1 Primary Regions of Operation
- Sword Coast: Waterdeep, Neverwinter, Baldur’s Gate.
- The North: Silverymoon, Loudwater.
- Dalelands and Cormanthor.

### 5.2 Public Perception
- To most common folk, they are rumor and legend.
- Tyrants and cults see them as a dangerous, if elusive, enemy.

### 5.3 Resources & Capabilities
- Network of safe houses and informants.
- Skilled diviners and bards for communication and intelligence.
- Limited access to powerful artifacts, heavily guarded and seldom deployed.

---
## 6. Relationships with Other Factions

- **Lords’ Alliance:** Cautious ally; Harpers push for ethics, Alliance for stability.
- **Zhentarim:** Ideological enemy; ongoing shadow war in many cities.
- **Emerald Enclave:** Frequent collaborators where natural balance is at stake.
- **Order of the Gauntlet:** Allied against obvious evil, but wary of zealotry.

---
## 7. Symbols, Colors & Iconography

- **Heraldry / symbol:** A silver harp on a field of twilight blue or star-flecked night.
- **Colors:** Silver, blue, and muted greys.
- **Motto / phrases:** "Balance in all things", "Watch, remember, act when needed."

---
## 8. Typical Agents & Tactics in Play

### 8.1 Typical Agents
- Soft-spoken bards, scholarly mages, ranger scouts, and morally driven rogues.

### 8.2 Tactics in a Campaign
- Approach PCs with quiet offers of information or aid.
- Provide quests that remove dangerous threats, expose plots, or rescue key figures.
- Rarely command; more often suggest and support.

---
## 9. Adventure Hooks

- A Harper contact asks the party to recover an artifact before a cult can seize it.
- Harpers need an escort for a bard carrying explosive political secrets.
- A cell has gone silent; the party must discover whether they were compromised.

---
## 10. Canon Notes & Variants

- **Baseline Canon:** Aligned with 5e-era Harpers, framed as slightly more cautious and subtle.
- **Campaign Variants:** Cells can be more radical, more conservative, or infiltrated. Record such changes under campaign-specific files.
- **Promotion Status:** Core Prime Harpers are fully canonical at Tier 1; deviations should be tracked per campaign.
```

## 1. Overview

**Full Name:** The Lords' Alliance  
**Common Names/Aliases:** The Alliance

**One-Sentence Summary:**
A coalition of powerful cities and rulers banded together for mutual defense, security, and stability along the Sword Coast and beyond.

**Longer Summary:**
The Lords' Alliance is a formal association of city-states and regional powers seeking to protect trade routes, maintain order, and fend off threats too great for any one nation. Though often noble in purpose, its decisions are driven by political interests and self-preservation as much as altruism.

---
## 2. Origins & History (High-Level)

- Formed when coastal powers recognized shared threats from orcs, monsters, cults, and foreign influence.
- Has expanded and contracted over the centuries as cities join or secede.
- Survived multiple crises in the 14th–15th centuries by adapting and reorganizing its leadership.

---
## 3. Ideology, Goals & Methods

### 3.1 Ideology
- Stability and prosperity of member states come first.
- Order, law, and controlled violence are preferable to chaos.

### 3.2 Goals
- Protect trade routes and key cities.
- Coordinate military responses to major threats.
- Maintain a favorable political balance among members.

### 3.3 Methods
- Diplomatic treaties, ultimatums, and collective action.
- Shared intelligence and military coordination.
- Occasional use of deniable assets (including adventurers).

---
## 4. Structure & Organization

### 4.1 Leadership
- Council of lords, rulers, and representatives from member cities and realms.
- Decisions often require consensus or majority agreements.

### 4.2 Internal Structure
- Envoys, military commanders, and appointed agents operating under Alliance mandates.

### 4.3 Membership
- Rulers, nobles, military officers, and trusted operatives.
- Adventurers are commonly contracted as outside specialists.

---
## 5. Presence & Influence

### 5.1 Primary Regions of Operation
- Sword Coast city-states.
- Western Heartlands.

### 5.2 Public Perception
- Generally seen as legitimate authority and protective force.
- Also viewed as political and self-serving by the cynical.

### 5.3 Resources & Capabilities
- Standing armies, fleets, and city defenses.
- Access to state treasuries and formal legal mechanisms.

---
## 6. Relationships with Other Factions

- **Harpers:** Cooperative but occasionally strained over methods and transparency.
- **Zhentarim:** Often hostile; sometimes pragmatically tolerant for trade.
- **Order of the Gauntlet:** Close ally in matters of defense and holy war.

---
## 7. Symbols, Colors & Iconography

- **Heraldry / symbol:** Varies by city; the Alliance itself is often represented by interlocking crests.
- **Colors:** Blues, golds, and local heraldic schemes.

---
## 8. Typical Agents & Tactics in Play

- Envoys, knights, magistrates, and contracted adventuring parties.
- Use legitimacy and resources as leverage.

---
## 9. Adventure Hooks

- PCs hired to secure a critical trade route against bandits and monsters.
- Alliance envoy requests help to negotiate with a volatile faction.

---
## 10. Canon Notes & Variants

- **Baseline Canon:** Matches 5e view as a pragmatic coalition.
- **Campaign Variants:** Alliance could become more authoritarian or fragmented.
```

**One-Sentence Summary:**
A ruthless mercenary and trade network using coin, blades, and quiet coercion to expand its influence across Faerûn.

**Longer Summary:**
The Zhentarim are a sprawling syndicate of mercenaries, traders, and criminals operating under a veneer of legitimacy. They offer protection, trade routes, and muscle—at the cost of dependence and eventual control. Their goals mix profit, power, and subtle territorial influence.

---
## 2. Origins & History (High-Level)

- Originated near the Moonsea as a militarized trade and conquest power.
- Survived leadership changes, magical catastrophes, and internal schisms.
- In the 1490s DR, they present a more "respectable" face while retaining ruthless methods.

---
## 3. Ideology, Goals & Methods

### 3.1 Ideology
- Strength, wealth, and control justify harsh methods.
- Loyalty is transactional and enforced by fear and reward.

### 3.2 Goals
- Expand trade monopolies and protection rackets.
- Embed agents in cities across the Sword Coast and beyond.
- Undermine rival powers, especially Harpers and hostile states.

### 3.3 Methods
- Mercenary contracts and protection services.
- Smuggling, blackmail, and quiet assassinations.
- Strategic alliances with villains, tyrants, and opportunistic rulers.

---
## 4. Structure & Organization

### 4.1 Leadership
- Powerful leaders with regional lieutenants.
- Authority maintained through fear, competence, and magical enforcement.

### 4.2 Internal Structure
- Cells, trade caravans, mercenary companies, and covert crews.

### 4.3 Membership
- Hardened mercenaries, spies, mages, and criminal experts.

---
## 5. Presence & Influence

### 5.1 Primary Regions of Operation
- Moonsea region.
- Sword Coast cities.
- Inland trade routes.

### 5.2 Public Perception
- Feared and mistrusted, but often tolerated for the "security" they provide.

### 5.3 Resources & Capabilities
- Armed companies, caravans, warehouses, and magical support.

---
## 6. Relationships with Other Factions

- **Harpers:** Active enemies in a long-term shadow conflict.
- **Lords’ Alliance:** Pragmatic adversaries or uneasy partners depending on city politics.
- **Cult of the Dragon:** Tools or rivals, depending on the scheme.

---
## 7. Symbols, Colors & Iconography

- **Heraldry / symbol:** Black serpent or dragon motifs, often coiled.
- **Colors:** Black, dark grey, gold accents.

---
## 8. Typical Agents & Tactics in Play

- Muscle, enforcers, "legitimate" caravan guards, and shadowy fixers.
- Offer deals with hidden hooks and escalating dependence.

---
## 9. Adventure Hooks

- PCs hired for a "simple" security job with murky ethics.
- PCs caught between Zhentarim and Harpers over control of a strategic asset.

---
## 10. Canon Notes & Variants

- **Baseline Canon:** Reflects 5e's more businesslike Zhents with a ruthless edge.
- **Campaign Variants:** May lean darker (near-evil empire) or more grey (pragmatic power).
```

## 1. Overview

**Full Name:** The Emerald Enclave  
**Common Names/Aliases:** The Enclave

**One-Sentence Summary:**
A loose alliance of druids, rangers, and wardens dedicated to preserving the natural balance of the world.

**Longer Summary:**
The Emerald Enclave operates across wildlands, borders, and even city fringes to ensure nature is neither trampled by civilization nor left to unchecked monstrous ruin. They intervene selectively, favoring balance over purity or dominance.

---
## 2. Origins & History (High-Level)

- Emerged from druidic circles worried about rampant expansion and magical disasters.
- Weathered eras of overgrowth, blight, and planar bleed-through.
- Now a quiet but potent influence where wilderness and civilization collide.

---
## 3. Ideology, Goals & Methods

### 3.1 Ideology
- Nature is not "good" or "evil"—it must be balanced.
- Civilization must respect limits, but cannot be entirely halted.

### 3.2 Goals
- Prevent ecological catastrophes and magical corruption.
- Maintain balance between settlements and the wild.

### 3.3 Methods
- Direct intervention against unnatural threats.
- Negotiation with local leaders about logging, hunting, and expansion.
- Strategic use of beasts, spirits, and druidic magic.

---
## 4. Structure & Organization

### 4.1 Leadership
- Loose councils of elders and powerful druids.

### 4.2 Internal Structure
- Circles and groves focused on specific regions or biomes.

### 4.3 Membership
- Druids, rangers, barbarians, and primal spellcasters.

---
## 5. Presence & Influence

### 5.1 Primary Regions of Operation
- Chult, High Forest, Dalelands, and frontier regions.

### 5.2 Public Perception
- Revered, tolerated, or feared depending on local history.

### 5.3 Resources & Capabilities
- Deep knowledge of terrain, beasts, and cycles.
- Ability to marshal natural forces against threats.

---
## 6. Relationships with Other Factions

- **Harpers:** Often aligned in opposing supernatural or tyrannical threats.
- **Lords’ Alliance:** Negotiates boundaries and land use.
- **Cult of the Dragon:** Active enemies when dragon activity disrupts balance.

---
## 7. Symbols, Colors & Iconography

- **Heraldry / symbol:** Stylized leaf or tree over a circle representing balance.
- **Colors:** Greens, browns, and earth tones.

---
## 8. Typical Agents & Tactics in Play

- Wilderness guides, solitary watchers, and druid circles.
- Provide quests to stop blights, abominations, or planar incursions.

---
## 9. Adventure Hooks

- PCs asked to investigate why beasts are fleeing a forest.
- Enclave seeks help halting a settlement that threatens sacred land.

---
## 10. Canon Notes & Variants

- **Baseline Canon:** Neutral, balance-focused Enclave.
- **Campaign Variants:** Some circles may be more radical or hostile to civilization.
```

### 4.1 Leadership
- Hierarchy of commanders, high priests, and champions.

- Chapters tied to strongholds and temples.

### 4.3 Membership

---
## 5. Presence & Influence

### 5.1 Primary Regions of Operation
- Sword Coast cities and frontier strongholds.

### 5.2 Public Perception
- Admired as heroes in threatened regions.

### 5.3 Resources & Capabilities
- Heavily armed troops, fortified chapterhouses, divine magic.

---
## 6. Relationships with Other Factions

- **Harpers:** Allies against obvious evil but clash over methods.
- **Lords’ Alliance:** Often ally in war; sometimes at odds over jurisdiction.

- **Colors:** Whites, silvers, and bold heraldic colors.

- Quest-givers for purging strongholds, undead, and cults.

---
## 9. Adventure Hooks

- PCs caught between the Order and a morally grey but vital local power.
