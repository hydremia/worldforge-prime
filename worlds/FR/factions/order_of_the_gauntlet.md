## 1. Overview

**Full Name:** The Order of the Gauntlet  
**Common Names/Aliases:** The Gauntlet

**One-Sentence Summary:**
A holy order of knights, clerics, and zealots dedicated to rooting out evil wherever it takes hold.

**Longer Summary:**
The Order of the Gauntlet gathers devout warriors and clergy who see the world as a battlefield between righteousness and corruption. They strive for decisive action against fiends, undead, and tyrants, sometimes at the cost of subtlety or political nuance.

---
## 2. Origins & History (High-Level)

- Formed by like-minded champions of Tyr, Torm, Helm, and similar deities.
- Gained notoriety during cult uprisings and demonic incursions along the Sword Coast.

---
## 3. Ideology, Goals & Methods

### 3.1 Ideology
- Evil must be confronted head-on, not appeased.
- Purity of purpose and honor in action are paramount.

### 3.2 Goals
- Destroy cults, fiends, undead, and tyrannical forces.
- Inspire common folk through acts of visible heroism.

### 3.3 Methods
- Military campaigns, purges, and crusades.
- Public trials and displays of justice.

---
## 4. Structure & Organization

### 4.1 Leadership
- Hierarchy of commanders, high priests, and champions.

### 4.2 Internal Structure
- Chapters tied to strongholds and temples.

### 4.3 Membership
- Paladins, clerics, fighters, and martially-inclined faithful.

---
## 5. Presence & Influence

### 5.1 Primary Regions of Operation
- Sword Coast cities and frontier strongholds.

### 5.2 Public Perception
- Admired as heroes in threatened regions.
- Viewed as rigid or dangerous zealots in more complex political climates.

### 5.3 Resources & Capabilities
- Heavily armed troops, fortified chapterhouses, divine magic.

---
## 6. Relationships with Other Factions

- **Harpers:** Allies against obvious evil but clash over methods.
- **Lords’ Alliance:** Often ally in war; sometimes at odds over jurisdiction.

---
## 7. Symbols, Colors & Iconography

- **Heraldry / symbol:** A metal gauntlet, often clenched.
- **Colors:** Whites, silvers, and bold heraldic colors.

---
## 8. Typical Agents & Tactics in Play

- Crusading paladins and cleric-led strike teams.
- Quest-givers for purging strongholds, undead, and cults.

---
## 9. Adventure Hooks

- PCs asked to assist in a crusade against a powerful necromancer.
- PCs caught between the Order and a morally grey but vital local power.

---
## 10. Canon Notes & Variants

- **Baseline Canon:** 5e-style righteous but sometimes inflexible order.
- **Campaign Variants:** Can slide toward fanaticism or tempered, pragmatic heroism.
