# Southwestern Basin — Southern Coast Subregion Dossier (Tier-2 World Canon Candidate)

This subregion is defined by terraced riverlands, proto-artificer workshops, fertile deltas, and a coastline dotted with drowned OCE remains. The balance of power is volatile but not anarchic: each player relies on the others to survive shifting magical landscapes, dead-magic pockets, and periodic technomantic failures.

## Primary Regional Powers

- **Velyr’s Gate** — Maritime-Artifice City-State
- **Thryvan Reach** — Highland–River Agrarian Bastion
- **The Isles of Deyadra** — Archipelagic Mystical Confederation

## Proximate Major Powers

- **Painted Cliffs Tribes** — Western nomadic cliff-dwelling coalition
- **Vaelward Marches** — Northern border-militant marshland realm
- **Selrya** — Ambitious arcano-bureaucratic state
- **The Fractured Crown** — Eastern successor-state remnants of dynastic ambitions

---

# 1. VELYR’S GATE

**Type:** Independent coastal city-state\
**Domains:** Maritime trade, technomancy, experimental artifice\
**Pantheon Resonance:** Arturion, Tessimir, Zephrix\
**Capital:** Velyr’s Gate\
**Identity:** A city built atop a partially dormant OCE ley-geyser, simultaneously empowered and endangered by volatile magical surges.

### Government & Structure

- **Tri-Guild Compact:** Rotating trade governance
- **Harbor Wardens:** Control access to ley-docked piers
- **Reliquarist Office:** Licenses OCE salvage operations
- **Coilwright Union:** Maintains unstable arcane pylon systems

### Foreign Relations

**Thryvan Reach:** Mutual dependency; tensions over river taxation.\
**Isles of Deyadra:** Trade partners but magical conflicts.\
**Painted Cliffs Tribes:** Minimal contact.\
**Vaelward Marches:** Deep distrust.\
**Selrya:** Economically entangled; fears annexation.\
**Fractured Crown:** Extorted through "protection" piracy.

### Core Conflicts

- Ley-pylon instability threatens the entire harbor.
- Guild intrigue may fracture the Tri-Compact.
- A reactivating drowned OCE ruin beneath the city.

# 2. THRYVAN REACH

**Type:** Highland–river agrarian state\
**Domains:** Agriculture, river-defense, civic cohesion\
**Pantheon Resonance:** Basturion, Vharûn, Mirethiel\
**Capital:** Stonereach\
**Identity:** The stabilizing agrarian center of the southern Basin; essential for food and infrastructure.

### Government & Structure

- **Wardline Orders:** Household militias protecting river routes
- **Council of Oath-Carvers:** Oversees land law and civic oaths
- **Floodkeepers:** Maintain levees and spillways

### Foreign Relations

**Velyr’s Gate:** Mutual dependency; cultural resentment.\
**Isles of Deyadra:** Cordial distance.\
**Painted Cliffs Tribes:** Respect through frontier diplomacy.\
**Vaelward Marches:** Strong defensive alliance.\
**Selrya:** Tension over grain tithe.\
**Fractured Crown:** Border skirmishes.

### Core Conflicts

- Pulse Road vibrations intensifying.
- Class tension rising between farmers and land-elites.
- Impossible tide-patterns detected upstream.

---

**Type:** Island confederation\
**Domains:** Druidic law, tidal prophecy, ancestral sea-rites\
**Pantheon Resonance:** Umbrayel, Aerathwyn, Emberith\
**Identity:** Mystical anchor of the southern coast; unpredictable but spiritually central.

### Foreign Relations

# REGIONAL POWER WEB EXPANSION (OPTION A)

This section expands the geopolitical, economic, magical, and military interactions within the Southern Coast subregion, with a sharper focus on how the surrounding **major powers** exert pressure on Velyr’s Gate, Thryvan Reach, and the Isles of Deyadra.

## Expanded Trade, Military, and Influence Networks

### **1. Selrya’s Influence Web (North / Central Power)**

Selrya is the dominant arcano-bureaucratic force in the mid‑Basin and views this southern subregion as both an asset and a liability.

**Economic Pressure:**

- Imposes fluctuating grain and tool tariffs on Thryvan Reach.
- Attempts to standardize Velyr’s technomantic production under Selryan regulations.
- Enforces aggressive port-record requirements on Gate merchants.

**Political Maneuvering:**

- Funds loyalist guilds in Velyr’s Gate to slowly leverage control of the Tri-Compact.
- Encourages local elites in the Reach to push toward Selryan-aligned agricultural policy.
- Tries repeatedly to force Deyadra into the arcane census (always rejected).

**Military Presence:**

- Maintains riverward detachments along the northern tributaries.
- Deploys "compliance battalions" to ensure enforcement of trade law.

**Magical Agenda:**

- Blames Deyadra for dream-path disruptions that affect Selryan records.
- Fears Velyr’s ley-geyser instability could threaten the entire Basin grid.

### **2. The Fractured Crown’s Shadow (Eastern Power)**

The Crown, a loose federation of aggressive successor-states and dynastic holdouts, pushes west and south whenever it senses weakness.

**Economic Disruption:**

- Targets Velyr’s Gate with extortionist "tribute flotillas."
- Distributes counterfeit tax tokens to destabilize Selryan influence.
- Attempts to redirect Reach grain caravans by force.

**Military Projection:**

- Small but frequent raids on the eastern lowlands of the Reach.
- Occasional naval incursions toward Deyadra, often repelled by tide-wardens.
- Attempts to infiltrate Velyr through mercenary guild fronts.

**Magical Risk:**

- Crown-aligned mages meddle with dormant OCE weaponry in coastal ruin-sites.
- Suspected of activating a drowned pylon near the southeastern shoals.

### **3. Vaelward Marches (Northwest Marshland Power)**

The Marches value discipline and border purity, seeing instability in the Basin as a long-term threat.

**Defensive Cooperation:**

- Strong coordination with Thryvan Reach for river and lowland defense.
- Joint militia patrols near the Painted Cliffs–Reach boundary.

**Suspicion and Mistrust:**

- Deep distrust of Velyr’s unregulated technomancy.
- Views Deyadra’s dream and tide practices as existential hazards.

**Political Pressure:**

- Attempts to enforce strict transit rules on Basin travellers passing north.

### **4. Painted Cliffs Tribes (Western Cliff-Dwelling Coalition)**

A culturally independent federation characterized by cliff settlements, color-coded clan markers, and long intertribal memory.

**Trade Dynamics:**

- Exchange cliff resins, pigments, and rare mineral powders with the Reach.
- Limited but symbolic trade with Deyadra during solstice rites.

**Cultural Influence:**

- Their shamans warn of “Pulse corruption” when passing near the Reach.
- They refuse Velyr’s attempts to establish trade-stations in cliff canyons.

**Boundary Tensions:**

- Tribes occasionally clash with Reach boundary wards over disputed grazing paths.

## Expanded Interactions Within the Subregion

### **Velyr’s Gate – Regional Hub of Volatile Power**

Velyr acts as the southern Basin’s commercial lung: drawing in resources, shipping out innovation. Its instability attracts both opportunity and predation.

**Downstream Effects:**

- Ley-flare nights disrupt Deyadran dream-paths.
- Magical surges destabilize river regulation in the Reach.
- Selrya uses these events to argue for regulatory control.

### **Thryvan Reach – Stabilizer and Gatekeeper**

The Reach is caught between duty and pressure, preserving agricultural lifelines while weathering political demands.

**Influence Routes:**

- Supplies the Painted Cliffs and Vaelward with stable grain shipments.
- Crucial to Selrya’s food security, making it a political battleground.
- Its Pulse Road resonance is monitored by Deyadra but dismissed by Selrya.

### **Isles of Deyadra – Spiritual and Tidal Counterweight**

Deyadra’s dream-law gives them disproportionate influence for their size, but isolationist tendencies limit continental power.

**Influence Routes:**

- Their druids warn of Basin-wide arcane imbalance.
- Tide-seers detect Crown naval motions before Selryan scouts.
- Their refusal to join any coalition destabilizes the local balance.

---

## Subregion-Wide Strategic Interactions

### **1. Trade Corridors**

- **Northflow Route:** Thryvan → Selrya river chain; heavily taxed.
- **Coastal Arc:** Velyr ↔ Deyadra; volatile but crucial for rare goods.
- **Cliffside Loop:** Reach ↔ Painted Cliffs; low volume but culturally important.

### **2. Military Chokepoints**

- **Stonereach Ford:** Controls central Basin movement.
- **Deyadra Straits:** Impossible to cross without tide-seer guidance.
- **Eastern Shoals:** Crown raiders frequently test boundaries.

### **3. Magical Geopolitics**

- **Ley-Geyser Nexus:** Velyr’s instability radiates pressure across the Basin.
- **Pulse Road Resonance:** Growing vibrations hint at deeper shifts.
- **Dream-Tide Interference:** Deyadra’s visions increasingly disrupted by land-based arcane pollution.

---

## Regional Stakes and Emerging Threats

- **Selryan Annexation Strategy:** Quietly building influence to fold Velyr and the Reach into its administrative map.
- **Fractured Crown Opportunism:** Exploiting instability to seize territory or relics.
- **Marchen Militarization:** Preparing for a defensive "containment regime" if instability spreads.
- **Deyadran Prophecy:** Sea-floor opening could reshape coastlines and power balances.
- **Pulse Road Awakening:** If it destabilizes, Reach infrastructure—and Basin food supply—collapse.

---

## New Summary

The Southern Coast is a **strategic hinge** between inland stability and coastal volatility. The three local powers form a fragile triangle caught between:

- Selrya’s bureaucratic domination,
- The Fractured Crown’s opportunistic aggression,
- The Vaelward Marches’ defensive rigidity,
- And the Painted Cliffs Tribes’ cultural autonomy.

The result: a region where political maneuvering, magical anomalies, and economic dependence collide—making it one of the most volatile and adventure-rich locales in the Southwestern Basin.

# INTERNAL POLITICS EXPANSION

## Velyr’s Gate — Factional Power Struggle

**1. Tri-Guild Compact Internal Blocs**

- **Mercantile Bloc (Green Sails):** Focused on open trade, favors Selryan partnership, opposes regulation of salvage markets.
- **Artifice Bloc (Coilwright Union Hardliners):** Prioritizes ley‑geyser exploitation, resists outside oversight, responsible for dangerous prototype cycles.
- **Dockside Syndicates (The Ropehands):** Smugglers and labor guilds controlling nighttime harbor traffic; unofficially tolerated to keep commerce moving.

**2. Salvager Cartels**

- Compete for rights to plunder drowned OCE infrastructure.
- Maintain secret maps of deep‑current ruin sites.
- Some rumored to collaborate with Fractured Crown relic-hunters.

**3. Selryan-Aligned Reform League**

- Bureaucrats and merchant elites seeking integration into Selryan trade law for “stability.”
- Opposed by both Coilwrights and Ropehands.

**4. The Pylon Custodiate (Esoteric Circle)**

- A technomantic priesthood claiming the ley‑geyser is alive.
- Wants to restrict all experiments, placing them under "ritual engineering."
- Considered zealots by most citizens.

---

## Thryvan Reach — Oaths, Land, and Social Tension

**1. Oath-Carver Legacy Families**

- Multi‑generational stonewright clans who maintain communal oaths.
- Conservative; resist Selryan influence and oppose changes to land allocation.

**2. Terrace Farmers’ Collective**

- Massive coalition of smallholders advocating for fair grain tithe negotiation.
- Increasingly militant; blame landholders and Selrya for exploitation.

**3. River Warders vs. River Engineers**

- **Warders:** Traditionalist defenders, follow Basturion’s protection doctrine.
- **Engineers:** Proto‑artificer innovators using water turbines, channel gates; accused of angering river spirits.

**4. The Pulse Road Observants**

- Mystics, druids, and a small minority of engineers who believe the Pulse Road is sentient.
- Want to keep all settlements away from the vibration epicenter.

---

## Isles of Deyadra — Circles in Quiet Conflict

**1. Tide-Seers (Dream Dominion)**

- Control predictive dream‑tides.
- Push for greater mainland engagement.
- Receive dream‑warnings of the sea-floor opening.

**2. Ember Orchard Wardens**

- Guardians of fire‑bloom groves.
- Militaristic; believe Emberith demands "transformative tests."
- Want to purge Crown influence violently.

**3. Deep Current Circle**

- Reclusive mystics who commune with abyssal currents far offshore.
- Believe the dream‑tide disruptions come from **below**, not from the mainland.

**4. Isolationist Grove Councils**

- Prefer minimal contact.
- Resist unifying leadership and reject all Selryan diplomacy.

# REGIONAL LANDMARKS & LOCATIONS

## Velyr’s Gate Region

**1. The Shatterpylon**

- The city’s massive ley‑geyser pylon.
- Rings with harmonic distortion during flare nights.
- Cracks in its foundation reveal OCE‑era glyphwork.

**2. The Drowned Quarter (Lowgate District)**

- Half‑submerged OCE neighborhood accessible only at low tide.
- New passages open unpredictably after magical surges.

**3. Geyserglass Shore**

- Beach where ley‑flare events fuse sand into green‑black glass.
- Used by Coilwrights for experimental lenses.

---

## Thryvan Reach Region

**1. Stonereach Ford**

- Major military chokepoint.
- Ancient stone causeway built over a Mirethiel‑aligned river node.

**2. The Pulse Scar**

- Region where Pulse Road vibrations are strongest.
- Soil resonates enough to disturb metal tools.

**3. The Oathvault Caves**

- Underground archives carved by Oath-Carver families.
- Store ancestral oath‑stones and legal precedents.

---

## Isles of Deyadra Region

**1. Ember Orchard of Kaldris**

- Grove where trees spontaneously ignite without burning.
- Produces emberfruit used in dream‑rituals.

**2. The shifting Atoll of Luryen**

- Island whose position changes after storms.
- Its lagoon contains bioluminescent tide‑spirits.

**3. The Tide‑Sealed Shrine of Veluna**

- Forbidden sanctuary.
- Tide remains motionless around it during prophecies.
